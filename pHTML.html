My Projects

HTML Website Building

I wanted a space that felt like me, but also somewhere I could show my work in a clean and professional way. For this reason I felt it was important for this website to be part portfolio, part playground.

Beginning my process in Figma I sketched out the initial wireframes and sitemape. This helped me to visualise the structure of the site, plan the appropriate navigation and decided how I wanted to balance content with negative space. I chose a minimalist style with a limited colour paleter, using pink as an accent in order to add warmth and individuality without overwhelming the overall aesthetic.

I then built the website with HTML and CSS (and a little JavaScript). I experimented with different hover effects and transitions which not only add interactivity but also helped to create a sense of polish. I used Flexbox and CSS Grids to structure content and ensure the site is responsive across devices.

While I wanted the site to feel professional, I also wanted it to feel like me. I included small details throughout the site in order to acheive this. This included the soft pink mentioned above, but also a hidden CV for Troy within my about page. I felt as though these touches were important to make the site feel both welcoming and memorable, while still presenting my work in a professional way.

This site gave me hands-on experience with design-to-code workflow, from creating wireframes to building a live site. It also taught me the value of planning ahead testing responsiveness and finding the correct palance between professionalism and personality.
